Bruno Fernandes, the midfield maestro?

For a major share of the last decade, Manchester United have been lacking in terms of a proper figure to pull the strings from behind in the midfield. A variety of names including Fletcher, Fellaini, Schneiderlin, Pogba, and lately the rising youngster McTominay have had the opportunity to operate from the sensitive spot, although the centre of the pitch always remained moderately dormant at Old Trafford. However, just a single match into his Mancunian career, Bruno Fernandes had shown the supporters that he is the kind of player the team have been looking for.

His 20 league goals were second best only to Benfica's Haris Seferovic. His 13 assists were second best only to Benfica's Pizzi.No one in the league was as crucial to their side in attack and he was the only star in the top flight to win Player of the Month twice.He was named Midfielder of the Month every month from October to April. And despite constant speculation surrounding his future, Fernandes has kept up his incredible form this term with eight goals and seven assists in just 15 games.He is comfortably outperforming his potential new team-mates when it comes to creating chances having played FEWER games.Renowned Portuguese football expert Felipe Dias says he has never seen a player capable of doing what the 25-year-old can.He told Sky Sports: "I have no doubt that Fernandes can thrive in the Premier League. This guy can play with and against the best of them.
